From Beginners To Bigshots

10 businesses you can start for less than $1,000

By National Federation of Independent Business
 – Contributing Writer
May 25, 2015, 3:17pm EDT
Updated May 25, 2015, 3:51pm EDT

For someone who’s never been an entrepreneur, starting a small business from the ground up sounds like a daunting — and expensive — endeavor.

But with a little creativity and a little cash, you can establish a successful small business that has potential to reap big rewards.

Depending on your skill set and experience, there are plenty of small business opportunities that cost very little to set up.

Here are just 10 small businesses you can start for $1,000 or less.

Virtual assistant

If you’re organized and punctual, have customer service experience, and possess a computer, phone, and reliable Internet connection, a virtual assistant business is within your reach. Virtual assistants help clients with administrative, marketing, IT, and other tasks via computer and phone. If you’d like a little training to help you sharpen your virtual assistant skills, there are plenty of affordable options.

Dog walker

A handful of leashes, some neighborhood flyers, and a little word of mouth is all you need to get your dog-walking business started. You may benefit from certification in dog handling, which you can find through a number of online resources, like Canine Club Academy. Bonus: You’ll enjoy tons of fresh air and get in great shape.

Airbnb

If you live in an area with a strong tourism market and you have space to spare, consider becoming an Airbnb rental. It’s an easy way to earn money on the side and can even become a consistent source of income. Here’s everything you need to know about hosting guests through Airbnb.

Bicycle tour guide

Do you like to get around town on two wheels? Do you live in an area where lots of visitors like to explore? If so, becoming a bicycle tour guide might be the perfect small business for you. Determine your tour itineraries through popular routes in your city, make sure your bike is in good working order, and start spreading the word! You may benefit from taking a first aid course.

Freelance writer or designer

If you have writing or design skills, becoming a freelancer can be a great small-business option. Chances are you’ve already established a professional network through your past experience. Set up a website to introduce potential clients to your services and start working! There are lots of online resources that can help link you with jobs to suit your skill set.

Tutoring

Put that English or education degree to good use as a tutor. You can offer your services for children, post-secondary students, or even new immigrants who are learning English as a second language. Consider renting a room at a local library or community center for your client sessions. Check out the National Tutoring Association to help you brush up on your teaching skills.

Indoor plant care

Just who looks after the plants in large buildings and office complexes, anyway? Indoor plant care is a little-known but extremely valuable service that requires very little overhead to start. If you have a green thumb, this could be an excellent business venture for you. Start by connecting with shopping malls, office buildings, hotels, and apartment complexes.

Freelance bookkeeper or money manager

If you have accounting or finance experience, becoming a freelance bookkeeper is another low-cost small business that’s relatively easy to start. Chances are you already possess the necessary financial certification. If you’re thinking of working with individuals and families to help them navigate daily money management issues, like balancing home budgets, saving for retirement, and paying bills, consider joining the American Association of Daily Money Managers.

Cake decorator

Indulge your sweet tooth and make money by becoming a cake decorator. Start by taking cake decorating classes, where you’ll also learn what equipment you’ll need for a home-based business. Then start baking! Be sure to take lots of photos of your creations and share them in an online portfolio. In addition to weddings, you can decorate cakes for birthday parties, graduations, retirements, holidays, and office functions.

Health and wellness consulting

If you have a degree in nutrition, personal training certification, or other health and wellness-based credentials, consider health and wellness consulting. Corporate wellness is a popular venture as more and more business owners are looking for ways to keep their teams healthy and fit. If you’re comfortable presenting in front of senior-level executives and implementing wellness plans for groups of people, this could be a great small business to try. Here’s a helpful how-to article about getting started in the corporate fitness market.
